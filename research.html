Research

My research interest is broadly equilibrium and non-equilibrium statistical physics to understand the physics of complex systems that spans from the phenomenology of turbulence to critical phenomena and phase transitions in complex environments. In the following, a brief description of the projects are given.

Entropy of ordered nematic phase in hard rods on lattice

Systems of hard rods have been of interest for a long time. This problem was first studied by Lars Onsager, who provided an exact calculation and argued for a phase transition at higher density from a disordered to a nematically ordered phase. Later, in 2007, Ghosh and Dhar (EPL) provided numerical Monte Carlo evidence showing the existence of another transition at high density, where the nematic order is broken and a high-density disordered (HDD) phase appears. In 2022, Shah et al. argued for the instability of the nematic phase and provided an understanding of why the second transition (observed only for k > 6) is entropy-driven. Similar models have also been studied on lattices such as the kagome lattice, which is of interest here.

Ranked percolation on 2D fractional Brownian surfaces

We study percolation on two-dimensional fractional Brownian(fBm) surfaces with different Hurst exponents. Our interest lies in the critical behaviour of this discontinuous phase transition as a function of the Hurst exponent H. In particular, we focus on the limit H → 0. We employ a universal gap-scaling approach to analyse the transition and its characteristics, following ideas developed in Universal Gap Scaling in Percolation(Fan, Meng, Liu & Saberi, Nat. Phys. 16, 455–461, 2020).

Voigt model of turbulence: a shell model study

Just like the regularity problem of the Navier–Stokes equations (for which the Clay Institute has a million dollars waiting), the regularity of the Voigt model of turbulence is also a well-posed mathematical problem (Oskolkov, 1973). There has been significant mathematical development of this model (Titi et al. 2006; Kalantarov et al. 2009). In this work, we study the Voigt model using a shell-model formulation to explore the high–Reynolds-number regime. Our focus is on the scaling behaviour of second-order structure functions (energy spectra) and dynamical scaling exponents. These behaviours are analysed in Pal, Kankaria & Ray (in preparation).

Conduction fluctuation in random resistor networks

Random resistor networks have been widely studied in the past. In the 1970s, much of the interest focused on the percolation threshold, with several works discussing scaling theory near the critical point pc. In the 1990s, it was realised that the current distribution near criticality in the bonds of the network is broad and cannot be characterised by a single exponent; instead, a multifractal description was proposed. The present study does not focus on criticality itself, but rather investigates conductance fluctuations away from the critical. The results are reported in B. Pal, Conduction fluctuations in random resistor networks with hyperuniform disorder (under review).
