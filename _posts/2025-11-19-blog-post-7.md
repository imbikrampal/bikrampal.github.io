We consider two–dimensional turbulence on a periodic square domain $\Omega = [0,L] \times [0,L]$. The grid contains \(N\) points in each direction, giving a uniform spacing $\Delta x = \frac{L}{N}.$ The physical gridpoint coordinates are $X_{ij} = \frac{iL}{N},    Y_{ij} = \frac{jL}{N}; i,j = 0,\ldots,N-1.$ All physical–space fields (vorticity, streamfunction, velocity, etc.) are defined on this grid. The solver stores the constants appearing in the 2D vorticity equation

$$
\partial_t \omega + \mathbf{u}\cdot\nabla\omega= \nu \nabla^2 \omega - \mu \omega + f_\omega,
$$

where \(\nu\) is the viscosity, \(\mu\) is the Ekman drag and \(dt\) is the timestep for temporal integration. The forcing is random and acts only on Fourier modes whose magnitude lies in a prescribed band $k_{\min} \le |\mathbf{k}| \le k_{\max}.$ The forcing amplitude is normalized so that the real–space RMS forcing attains a desired value.  
All parameters are stored for constructing the forcing operator. Due to periodicity, admissible Fourier modes are indexed by $n_x,  n_y \in \{-N/2+1,\ldots,N/2\}.$ The corresponding wavenumbers are $k_x = \frac{2\pi}{L}  n_x, 
k_y = \frac{2\pi}{L}  n_y,$
with magnitude and squared magnitude

$$
|k| = \sqrt{k_x^2 + k_y^2}.
$$

The streamfunction in Fourier space is obtained from vorticity via $\psi(\mathbf{k}) = - \frac{\omega(\mathbf{k})}{k^2}$. Since \(k^2 = 0\) at the zero mode, the solver uses a modified quantity

$$
k^2_{\text{safe}} =
\begin{cases}
	k^2, & (n_x,n_y) \neq (0,0), \\[4pt]
	1, & (n_x,n_y)   = (0,0),
\end{cases}
$$

so that divisions never involve literal zeros. The solver analytically enforces \(\psi(0,0)=0\). Nonlinear terms such as \(\mathbf{u}\cdot\nabla\omega\) generate unresolved modes that would alias back into the resolved spectrum.  
The spectral \(2/3\) rule applies the Galerkin projection

$$
|n_x| \le \frac{2}{3} \frac{N}{2},
\qquad
|n_y| \le \frac{2}{3} \frac{N}{2},
$$

and sets all other modes to zero following each nonlinear product.  
This ensures mathematical consistency of the spectral method. The solver initializes the vorticity field in Fourier space  with $\omega(\mathbf{k}, 0) = 0,$ which serves as the initial dynamical state to be advanced in time.
The final call init_forcing() constructs the random forcing process in Fourier space.

## Computation $\hat{\psi}$, $\hat{u}$ and $\hat{v}$
The code implements precisely the relation $\psi(\mathbf{k}) = -\frac{\omega(\mathbf{k})}{k^2},  \psi(0)=0.$ Velocity is recovered from the streamfunction via $u = \partial_y \psi,   v = -\partial_x \psi.$ Fourier differentiation gives $\widehat{\partial_y\psi} = i k_y \psi(\mathbf{k}), \widehat{\partial_x\psi} = i k_x \psi(\mathbf{k}),$
so that the spectral velocity is

$$
u(\mathbf{k}) = i k_y \psi(\mathbf{k}), \qquad
v(\mathbf{k}) = -i k_x \psi(\mathbf{k}).
$$

These expressions and guarantees divergence-free velocity $k_x u(\mathbf{k}) + k_y v(\mathbf{k}) = 0$ which gives an incompressible flow with $\nabla\cdot \mathbf{u}=0$. 

## Scaled Forcing

The forcing is constructed in vorticity space as a frozen random spectrum supported on a prescribed wavenumber band. Define the forcing set $\mathcal{B} = \{\mathbf{k} : k_{\min} \le |\mathbf{k}| \le k_{\max}\}.$
The solver implements this via a Boolean mask selecting only these modes. Each mode in \(\mathcal{B}\) receives a complex number of unit magnitude,

$$
\hat{g}(\mathbf{k}) = e^{i\theta(\mathbf{k})},
\qquad \theta(\mathbf{k}) \sim \mathrm{Uniform}[0,2\pi),
$$

yielding an isotropic, phase-randomized forcing spectrum. Real physical-space forcing requires hermitian symmetry i.e., $\hat{f}_\omega(-\mathbf{k}) 
= \overline{\hat{f}_\omega(\mathbf{k})}.$ The solver enforces this via reflection and averaging:

$$
\hat{f}_\omega(\mathbf{k}) 
= \tfrac{1}{2} \left(\hat{g}(\mathbf{k}) 
+ \overline{\hat{g}(-\mathbf{k})} \right),
$$

ensuring that the inverse FFT yields a real field. The zero mode is suppressed $\hat{f}_\omega(0,0) = 0,$ since forcing the mean vorticity would imply infinite circulation input. The forcing is finally projected onto the \(2/3\)-dealiased region $\hat{f}_{\omega,\text{final}}(\mathbf{k})
= \hat{f}_\omega(\mathbf{k})   \chi_{\text{dealias}}(\mathbf{k}),$
eliminating all non-dealiased modes. Thus the frozen forcing spectrum stored by the solver is

$$
	\hat{f}_{\omega,\text{frozen}}(\mathbf{k}) =
	\begin{cases}
		e^{i\theta(\mathbf{k})}, & \mathbf{k}\in\mathcal{B}
		\text{ and dealiased},\\
		0, & \text{otherwise}.
    \end{cases}
$$

Beginning with $f_\omega(\mathbf{k}) = \hat{f}_{\omega,\text{frozen}}(\mathbf{k}),$ the corresponding forcing streamfunction is computed using $\psi_f(\mathbf{k}) = -\frac{f_\omega(\mathbf{k})}{k^2}.$ The physical-space forcing velocities follow from the streamfunction relations $f_x = \partial_y\psi_f, f_y = -\partial_x\psi_f,$ which in Fourier space yield $\widehat{f_x} = i k_y \psi_f(\mathbf{k}),  \widehat{f_y} = -i k_x \psi_f(\mathbf{k}).$ Inverse transforms then give the real-space fields. The RMS magnitude of the forcing velocity is

$$
\mathrm{RMS}
= \sqrt{\frac{1}{L^2}\int_\Omega (f_x^2 + f_y^2)  dA}
\approx
\sqrt{\frac{1}{N^2}\sum_{i,j}(f_x^2 + f_y^2)}.
$$

If this RMS value is nonzero, a scaling factor $\lambda = \frac{A_{\text{target}}}{\mathrm{RMS}}$ is applied so that the resulting forcing satisfies $\sqrt{\langle (\lambda f_x)^2 + (\lambda f_y)^2\rangle}
= A_{\text{target}}$ where $A_{\text{target}}$ is the forcing amplitude tuned by user. Thus the final scaled vorticity-forcing spectrum returned each timestep is $\hat{f}_{\omega}^{\text{scaled}}(\mathbf{k})=\lambda \hat{f}_{\omega,\text{frozen}}(\mathbf{k}).$ The forcing is constructed as an isotropic, random, band-limited spectrum following the method of Eswaran and Pope.

## Nonlinear Term

The nonlinear term in the 2D vorticity equation is

$$
\mathcal{N}(\omega)
= \mathbf{u}\cdot\nabla\omega
= u \partial_x \omega + v \partial_y \omega .
$$

The program computes this term in physical space and returns its Fourier transform with proper de-aliasing. It starts with recovering the stream function and velocity. The streamfunction in Fourier space is obtained from $\psi(\mathbf{k}) = -\frac{\omega(\mathbf{k})}{k^2},$ from which the velocity components follow $u(\mathbf{k}) = i k_y \psi(\mathbf{k}),  v(\mathbf{k}) = -i k_x \psi(\mathbf{k}).$ Using inverse FFTs $u(x,y) = \mathcal{F}^{-1}[u(\mathbf{k})], v(x,y) = \mathcal{F}^{-1}[v(\mathbf{k})],$ and $\omega(x,y) = \mathcal{F}^{-1}[\omega(\mathbf{k})].$ Then we compute vorticity derivatives. Spectral differentiation gives $\partial_x \omega(\mathbf{k}) = i k_x \omega(\mathbf{k}), \partial_y \omega(\mathbf{k}) = i k_y \omega(\mathbf{k}),$ and after inverse FFT,

$$
\omega_x = \mathcal{F}^{-1}[i k_x \omega(\mathbf{k})],  \omega_y = \mathcal{F}^{-1}[i k_y \omega(\mathbf{k})].
$$

In physical space, $\mathcal{N}(x,y) = u \omega_x + v \omega_y .$ In Fourier space $\widehat{\mathcal{N}}(\mathbf{k})=\mathcal{F}\!\left[\mathcal{N}(x,y)\right].$ Using the $2/3$-rule mask $D(\mathbf{k})$,
$\widehat{\mathcal{N}}_{\mathrm{dealias}}(\mathbf{k})
= D(\mathbf{k}) \widehat{\mathcal{N}}(\mathbf{k}).$ The Fourier-space vorticity equation is

$$
\partial_t \omega(\mathbf{k})
= -\widehat{\mathbf{u}\cdot\nabla \omega}
- \nu k^{2}\omega
- \mu\omega
+ \hat{f}.
$$

Thus the program returns $-\widehat{\mathcal{N}}_{\mathrm{dealias}}(\mathbf{k}),$ the correct nonlinear contribution to the RHS.

## RK2 time stepping

We solve the Fourier-space PDE

$$
\partial_t \omega(\mathbf{k},t)
= -\nu k^2 \omega
- \mu \omega
+ N(\mathbf{k},t)
+ f(\mathbf{k},t),
$$

where $N(\mathbf{k}) = -\widehat{\mathbf{u}\cdot\nabla\omega}.$ Define the linear operator $L(\mathbf{k}) = \nu k^2 + \mu,$ whose integrating factor is $e^{Lt}$. Now we precompute exponentials $E_{1/2} = e^{-L\Delta t/2}, E_1 = e^{-L\Delta t}.$ Then perform  

1. First Nonlinear Evolution : $N_1 = N(\omega) + f .$  
2. Midpoint State : $\omega^\star= E_{1/2}\!\left(\omega + \frac{\Delta t}{2} N_1\right).$  
3. Second Nonlinear Evaluation : $N_2 = N(\omega^\star) + f,$ with forcing re-evaluated if time-dependent or stochastic.  
4. Final Update : $\displaystyle \omega^{n+1}= E_1 \omega + \Delta t E_{1/2} N_2 .$  

This scheme is second-order accurate and integrates the stiff linear part exactly.

The program returns the physical fields

$$
 \left( \omega(x,y),\; u(x,y),\; v(x,y)  \right).
$$

These represent the real--space vorticity and velocity associated with the solver's current spectral state.

## Initial Condition

The routine constructs an initial vorticity field $\omega(x,y,t=0)$ whose Fourier coefficients are random but restricted to a narrow annulus in wavenumber space. This produces a smooth, band--limited random field commonly used as an
initial condition in 2D turbulence simulations. If the user does not specify the central wavenumber $k_0$, it is taken from
the forcing band $k_0 = \frac{1}{2}(k_{\min}+k_{\max}), \text{width} = \frac{1}{2}(k_{\max}-k_{\min}).$ Otherwise the user--supplied $k_0$ is used and the width defaults to~1.
The active spectral region is the annulus $\mathcal{B}
=  \left\{\mathbf{k} :   \lvert |\mathbf{k}| - k_0 \rvert \le \text{width} \right\}.$ Let $k = |\mathbf{k}| = \sqrt{k_x^2+k_y^2}$.  

Define

$$
\chi_{\mathcal{B}}(\mathbf{k})
=
\begin{cases}
	1, & \lvert k - k_0\rvert \le \text{width},\\
	0, & \text{otherwise}.
\end{cases}
$$

If no grid points satisfy this condition, the routine terminates with an error. The code generates complex Gaussian noise, $\eta(\mathbf{k})= \eta_1(\mathbf{k})+ i \eta_2(\mathbf{k}), \eta_1,\eta_2 \sim \mathcal{N}(0,1),$ and applies the spectral mask $\hat{\omega}(\mathbf{k})= A \eta(\mathbf{k}) \chi_{\mathcal{B}}(\mathbf{k}).$ To obtain a real physical field, the coefficients must satisfy $\hat{\omega}(-\mathbf{k})= \overline{\hat{\omega}(\mathbf{k})}.$
The routine enforces this via the symmetrized assignment

$$
\hat{\omega}(\mathbf{k})
\;\leftarrow\;
\frac{1}{2} \left(
\hat{\omega}(\mathbf{k})+ \overline{\hat{\omega}(-\mathbf{k})}
 \right),
$$

and sets the zero mode to zero, $\hat{\omega}(0,0)=0,$ so that the initial circulation vanishes.
The current RMS velocity is

$$
U_{\mathrm{rms}}^{\mathrm{current}}
=
\sqrt{
	 \left\langle
	u^2 + v^2
	 \right\rangle
}.
$$

If the user specifies a target RMS velocity
$U_{\mathrm{rms}}^{\mathrm{target}}$, the spectrum is rescaled by $\lambda=\frac{U_{\mathrm{rms}}^{\mathrm{target}}}{U_{\mathrm{rms}}^{\mathrm{current}}}, \hat{\omega}_{\text{final}}(\mathbf{k})=\lambda \hat{\omega}(\mathbf{k}).$ If the solver defines a de-aliasing mask $\chi_{\text{dealias}}(\mathbf{k})$,
the initial condition is projected onto the admissible spectral region $\hat{\omega}_{\text{final}}(\mathbf{k})\;\leftarrow\;\hat{\omega}_{\text{final}}(\mathbf{k}) \chi_{\text{dealias}}(\mathbf{k}).$ The program returns useful diagnostic information $U_{\mathrm{rms}}^{\mathrm{current}},U_{\mathrm{rms}}^{\mathrm{target}},\lambda,k_0,\text{width},\{\mathbf{k}\in\mathcal{B}\}.$
The mathematical form of the constructed vorticity field is

$$
	\omega_0(x,y)=\mathcal{F}^{-1} \left(\lambda A \eta(\mathbf{k}) \chi_{\mathcal{B}}(\mathbf{k}) \chi_{\text{dealias}}(\mathbf{k})\right).
$$

This is a random, band--limited, real vorticity field suitable for initiating
2D turbulence simulations.

## Parameters

The solver advances the two-dimensional vorticity equation 
\(\partial_t \omega + \mathbf{u}\cdot\nabla\omega = \nu\nabla^2\omega - \mu\omega + f\)
on a square periodic domain. The simulation parameters are
\(N=256\), \(L=2\pi\), \(\nu=10^{-5}\), \(\mu=0.02\), and a timestep \(\Delta t = 10^{-3}\).
A statistically stationary flow is maintained using a random band-limited forcing of amplitude
\(0.03\) applied at wavenumber \(k_f = 5\). The total simulated time is \(T=1000\),
corresponding to \(10^6\) timesteps. The initial condition is a band-limited random vorticity
field centered at \(k_0 = 2\) and scaled so that the initial RMS velocity satisfies
\(U_{\mathrm{rms}} = 0.1\).

A function compute_diagnostics evaluates all standard quadratic measures used to
quantify two-dimensional turbulence. The kinetic energy is
\(\mathrm{KE} = \tfrac12\langle u^2 + v^2\rangle\), while the enstrophy is
\(\mathcal{Z} = \tfrac12\langle\omega^2\rangle\). Palinstrophy, defined as
\(P = \langle|\nabla\omega|^2\rangle\), is computed spectrally using
\(\omega_x = i k_x \omega(\mathbf{k})\) and \(\omega_y = i k_y\omega(\mathbf{k})\).
It provides the enstrophy-dissipation rate via \(\epsilon_\omega = \nu P\).
The forcing contributes an enstrophy injection rate \(\epsilon = \langle\omega f\rangle\),
where \(f\) is obtained by inverse transforming the spectral forcing field. These quantities are standard invariants used in the analysis of 2D turbulence.

Several diagnostic Reynolds numbers are computed. The RMS velocity is
\(U_{\mathrm{rms}} = \sqrt{\langle u^2 + v^2\rangle}\). The large-scale or ``box'' Reynolds
number is \(Re_{\mathrm{box}} = U_{\mathrm{rms}}L/\nu\), while the forcing-scale Reynolds
number is \(Re_f = U_{\mathrm{rms}}/(\nu k_f)\), based on the forcing length scale
\(\ell_f = 1/k_f\). Velocity gradients obtained spectrally yield
\(\langle|\nabla u|^2\rangle = \langle u_x^2 + u_y^2 + v_x^2 + v_y^2\rangle\), from which the
Taylor microscale \(\lambda = U_{\mathrm{rms}}/\sqrt{\langle|\nabla u|^2\rangle}\)
and Taylor Reynolds number \(Re_\lambda = U_{\mathrm{rms}}\lambda/\nu\) are determined. These diagnostic quantities follow standard turbulence theory.

## Time-Loop Outputs
Every 200 steps the solver performs the following tasks  saves the full simulation state 
\((\omega, u, v, \omega_{\mathbf{k}}, t)\), prints all diagnostic quantities, prints all Reynolds numbers, monitors the evolution of enstrophy, energy, palinstrophy, and the enstrophy injection rate. This produces a complete time-evolution dataset suitable for spectral analysis, cascade studies, and steady--state detection.
The solver integrates the vorticity equation

$$
\partial_t \omega + \mathbf{u}\cdot\nabla\omega
= \nu \Delta \omega - \mu \omega + f.
$$

The numerical scheme employs a spectral discretization, integrating-factor RK2 time stepping, random band-limited forcing, dealiasing using the \(2/3\) rule. At regular intervals the solver computes

$$
\begin{aligned}
	\mathrm{KE} &= \tfrac12 \langle u^2 + v^2 \rangle,  \mathcal{Z} = \tfrac12 \langle \omega^2 \rangle, \\
	P &= \langle |\nabla\omega|^2 \rangle,  \epsilon = \langle \omega f \rangle, \\
	Re_{\mathrm{box}} &= \frac{U_{\mathrm{rms}} L}{\nu}, 
	Re_f = \frac{U_{\mathrm{rms}}}{\nu k_f},  Re_\lambda = \frac{U_{\mathrm{rms}}^2}{\nu \sqrt{\langle |\nabla u|^2 \rangle}}.
\end{aligned}
$$

The full flow state is written to file for later analysis.
